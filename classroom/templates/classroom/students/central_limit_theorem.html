We find that as we increase our dice rolls, the probability evens out for each number on our dice. Why is that? The Law of Large Numbers tells us that when we increase the number of times we repeat an unpredictable experiment we will see a pattern than resembles the actual probabilities of getting a given outcome.

What?

Well look at the example we just showed you, when we increased the times we rolled our dice, the probability of landing each number one our dice approached 1/6th.

Central Limit Theorem¶

The Central Limit Theorem is closely related to the law of large numbers. The central limit theorem stats that the sampling distribution of our sample means will approach a normal distribution (resemble a bell curve) as the sample size increases regardless of the shape of the distribution of the population. What this means is that as we roll our dice more and more times, the distribution of the mean will start to resemble a bell curve. The average will lean closer to 3.

Let's show this using code. We are going to start off by creading a random uniform distribution using numpy such that the lower boundary is -10, the upper boundary is 25the highest is 40 and size of 10000

So what did we do and what does this mean. Well going back to the definition. If we take a large enough sample from the population,the sampling distribution of the mean of a variable regardless of the shape of the distribution of the population, will approximate a normal distribution or in other words it will resemble a bell curve. In this case we continued taking random samples of 25,700 times, and found that the mean of these samples started to resemble a bell curve.

Why is this important?¶

Well, as stated in your notes, it is close to impossible to collect the data of the entire population. We can instead gather a subset of our data from the population and use statistics to draw conclusions about the population.

This assumption of the Central Limit Theorem helps us overcome the problem of the data belonging to a population that is not normally distribution. We can quantify the likelihood that our sample will deviate from the population and that the sample will be representative of the population.

We can use the concepts of confidence interval and hypothesis testing to know the likelihood that for example obsvervations likely like within one standard deviation from the population mean.

How?

Let's unpack this graph. We call this the 68%-95%-99.7% rule. According to this rule 68% of the data is within 1 standard devation of the mean, 95% within 2 standard deviation so the mean and 99.7% within 3 standard deviations of the mean. This rule is typically associated with normal distributions. We can use this rule to easily obtain quick estimates.

Note however, the CLT doesn't talk about the distribution of the data from the sample, it talks about the sample means and sample proportions of many different random samples drawn from the same population.

CLT assumptions

This is however all dependent on two key assumptions; Firstly that the value sampled are independent of each other. In other words, is there any reason to think that the data values might affect each other. Secondly, this assumes that the sample size is large enough.We should remember to check whether these assumptions are reasonable. We can try check the reasonableness of these assumptions by asking whether the data came from a randomizatino condition which was not biased and where data sampled was adequately representative of the population.
