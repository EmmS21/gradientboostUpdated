Home Details

Objectives

Before entering a new field it is important to understanding the fundamentals. For this course we decided to focus heavily on statistics to help you start building the foundations, helping you better understand probability, bayesian thinking and inferential and descriptive statistics. We also thought it necessary to introduce you to SQL to better understand how to both build and query data from relational databases. When it comes to programming we place more emphasis on logic and helping students understand not just how to do something, but the why and the understanding of the logic behind each solution.

This program is designed for students who have limited to no experience with programming and statistics, while intense, it is achievable if you invest enough time and fully leverage your mentor's expertise. You will have weekly tutorial with a mentor to ask questions based on what you have completed in a given week and access to proprietary study material.

We have designed this course to be valuable to students who want to start the process of pursuing a career in data analysis, data science and data engineering, people who want to build a high level understanding of the data science process and develop some level of practical understanding of what data scientists/analysts/engineers do. Lastly, in order to help you communicate technical projects to a non technical audience, you will be required to explain your project to a panel or mentors and fellow students. Through this process you will be expected to communicate your work, along with the expected impact and recommendations derived from your findings.

Eligibility

The only criteria for pursuing this course is that you must have access to WiFi and a working laptop.

Course Outline

    • Introduction to Statistics for Data Science

    In the statistics part of the curriculum you will cover topics such as rules of probability, different types of probability distributions and inferential statistics to name a few.

    Introduction to Python for programming

    The focus on this part of the curriculum is to help you build an understanding of core programming principles using Python. Topics that are covered will include control flows and conditionals, file handling, Pandas and NumPy and some Object Oriented Programming principles.

    Introduction to SQL

    The SQL part of the curriculum focuses on giving you a practical understanding of learning how to communicate and manipulate relational databases using SQL.

    Introduction to Data Visualization

    Through this part of the curriculum we will focus on giving you a practical understanding of using Python libraries such as Matplotlib as Seaborn to create interesting visualizations to tell stories using datasets.

    Introduction to Linear Algebra for Data Science

    In this module we will focus on getting a better understanding of vectors, linear combinations and spans of vectors, linear dependence and matrix vector products to name a few topics.

    Introduction to Machine Learning

    For the machine learning course the core focus will be on getting both an intuitive and practical understanding of regression and classification algorithms ranging from linear regression to decision trees. We will also delve into a bit more detail about the bias variance trade off. You will go through interactive Jupyter Notebooks to understand how to build these algorithms from scratch without using the Sci-Kit Learn library.

    Building your first project

    Applying Python and SQL concepts you have learnt practically will help cement knowledge you gained from the curriculum. You will be required to build a pre-defined project and present this to both your mentors and peers. In addition to helping you build your technical skills, problem solving capacity and ability to understand a business problem, this will help you develop your communication skills and ability to present technical findings to an audience with varying degrees of technical expertise.

    Building your second project

    By now you should be ready to use the skills you have learnt to create a business case study, define a clear and quantifiable business problem and then proceed to use the skills you have acquired to build a solution, measure its potential benefits and make suggestions. The best thing about projects is that this is your chance to shine by going the extra mile to leverage the assistance of your mentors and The Gradient Boost community to execute a jawdropping project.

    Case Study and Group Project

    Being able to break down a case study to smaller tasks, assigning them to different team members and collaborating to build a practical solution is a core skill you will acquire through this project. After you decide on a business problem, we will have discussions to understand what tasks will be required to solve this problem and then as a team you will assign tasks to different team members. This process will add more accountability to each person in the team and help you better understand how to navigate different work styles.
